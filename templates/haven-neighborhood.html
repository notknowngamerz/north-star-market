Maplewood Heights

The neighborhood that defines gracious living in our area. Century-old oaks canopy the streets, and the homes — mostly colonials and tudors from the 1920s–40s — have been lovingly maintained or thoughtfully updated.

  • CharacterHeritage homes, tree-lined streets
  • SchoolsTop-rated public and private options
  • WalkabilityVillage center with shops and dining
  • Average home price$1.9M – $3.2M

Cedar Ridge

A neighborhood in evolution. Original craftsman bungalows sit alongside award-winning contemporary builds, connected by trails that wind through cedar groves and open onto a vibrant village green.

  • CharacterMix of restored and new construction
  • OutdoorsTrail access, community garden, park
  • DiningFarm-to-table restaurants, artisan cafés
  • Average home price$1.4M – $2.6M

Ridgewood

For those who value space and serenity above all. Ridgewood's generous lots — most over an acre — offer a level of privacy that's increasingly rare. The homes here are estates in the truest sense.

  • CharacterGrand estates, gated entries, mature landscaping
  • PrivacyLarge lots, wooded buffers, private drives
  • AmenitiesCountry club, equestrian trails, lake access
  • Average home price$2.5M – $5.8M

Brookhaven

The quiet achiever. Brookhaven has a humility that belies its charm — a creek runs through the heart of it, and the homes, while more modest in scale, overflow with character and warmth.

  • CharacterCottages, bungalows, and garden homes
  • NatureCreek-side trails, bird sanctuaries
  • CommunityActive neighborhood association, block parties
  • Average home price$950K – $1.5M
